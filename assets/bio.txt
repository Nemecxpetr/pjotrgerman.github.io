English
1. Main universal bio

Petr Nìmec develops a sound-based practice at the intersection of composition, performance, installation, and participatory situations. As a sound artist, composition student, and performer, he explores porous relationships between the body, technical systems, and the environment.

His work is grounded in an interest in sound as both a material force and a relational medium — one that can activate collective listening, intensify the bodily experience of vibration, and open new ways of perceiving time, space, memory, and presence.

Through imaginatively composed situations, he seeks ways to connect inner and outer dimensions of human perception and to create deeper experiences of shared attention and encounter.

2. Short festival version

Petr Nìmec is a sound artist, composition student, and performer. His work moves between composition, performance, installation, and participatory formats through which he explores relationships between the body, the environment, and technology. He is interested in collective listening, the corporeality of vibration, and the potential of sound as a medium that reshapes the perception of time, space, memory, and presence.

3. Longer conceptual version

Petr Nìmec develops a sound-based practice at the intersection of composition, performance, installation, and participatory situations. In his work, sound is understood not only as an acoustic phenomenon, but as a material force and a relational medium through which porous boundaries between the human body, technical systems, and the environment can be explored.

His long-term interests include collective listening, the corporeality of vibration, and the relationships between space and body, time and memory, presence and its traces. He is interested in how imaginatively composed situations can transform involuntary hearing into conscious listening, and how they can create conditions for more intense experiences of encounter.

His practice seeks to connect inner and outer landscapes of perception and to develop forms of shared attention in which imagination, participation, and sensitivity to environment become tools for a deeper lived experience.
